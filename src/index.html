Peter Sagan, who is he?

Peter Sagan (Slovak pronunciation: [ˈpɛtɛr ˈsaɡan]; born 26 January 1990) is a Slovak professional road bicycle racer, who currently rides for UCI WorldTeam Bora–Hansgrohe.[6] Sagan had a successful junior cyclo-cross and mountain bike racing career, winning the Junior Mountain Bike World Championship in 2008, before moving to road racing. Sagan is considered one of cycling's greatest talents, having earned many prestigious victories,[7][8] including three consecutive World Championships, one European Championship, two Paris–Nice stages, seven Tirreno–Adriatico stages, one in the Tour de Romandie, three and the overall classification in the Tour de Pologne, a record seventeen stages and the overall in the Tour of California,[9] and another fifteen in the Tour de Suisse. He has won a number of classics, including the Monument races Paris-Roubaix and Tour of Flanders, three Gent–Wevelgem races and E3 Harelbeke, together with seventeen stages in Grand Tours: twelve at the Tour de France, four at the Vuelta a España, and one at the Giro d'Italia. After having become the first rider to win the points classification of the Tour de France on his first five attempts, he went on to win it a record seven times, in 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2018 and 2019.

Career

Peter was the last child of his parents, who have two more sons and a daughter. Peter was brought up older sister, since his parents devoted most of the day to the maintenance of a small grocery store. Their middle son, Jurai, Peter's brother, also became a cyclist, and is in the same team. Peter started cycling at the age of 9 in his native Zilina. Sagan was noticed for his interesting style: tennis shoes and a T-shirt. He also attracted a lot of attention by appearing at the Slovak Championship with sister's bike. Then Peter mistakenly sold his bike, and the spare sponsored bike could not be received in time. He won that race, despite the fact that he was in the usual non-professional a bike with poor brakes and grip. Successfully performing both on the highway and on rough terrain, in 2008 he became the world champion among juniors in cross-country. In 2010, Sagan obtained a professional license and signed a two-year contract with Liquigas-Cannondale, which was renewed for another year in April. In January, Slovak started on the Tour Down Ander, where in the second stage he fell very unsuccessfully. But with 17 stitches applied, he finished twice in top five in the remaining stages. On March 10, Sagan won his first race, Stage 3 Paris - Nice. And the victory in the 5th stage allowed him to win the sprint classification. On the Tour of Romandy, Sagan won one stage, and finished second twice more. In May, he won two sprint finishes on the Tour of California. where he again excelled in points. During the season, Sagan finished in the top ten more than once. prestigious races, including second place at the Montreal Grand Prix. These achievements made him the main opening of the season [2]. In 2011, the Slovak also won a number of victories at the stages of multi-day races, two of which he managed to win: Tour of Poland and Giro di Sardinia. In September, Sagan made his grand tour debut, starting at the Vuelta. and won 3 stages at once - more than anyone else. In early spring 2012, he won several stages of multi-day events and got into the top five at Milan - San Remo and Tour of Flanders. At the 2012 Tour de France, Sagan won 3 of the first 7 stages. More wins on the Tour he did not win, but the stock of the first week and finishes in high positions of the late lowland stages Sagan had enough to conquer the green jersey.
